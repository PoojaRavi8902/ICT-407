Phase 2 — Short Lecture (10 minutes)

Key teaching points (deliver in ~10 minutes)

  1. Introduce the role of network devices: modem, hub, switch, bridge, router, gateway and NIC — show a physical photo or diagram if available.
  2. Explain differences: hub broadcasts to all ports → inefficient and insecure; switch forwards to MAC address → efficient; router connects networks using IP addresses.
  3. Briefly compare dial‑up modem vs broadband modem (speed, always‑on, blocking telephone line vs not).
  4. Explain IP vs MAC with simple analogy: IP = house address (location), MAC = person name (device identity). Show example addresses (192.168.1.10 and 00:1A:2B:3C:4D:5E).
  5. Summarise wireless options: Wi‑Fi for larger area & internet access; Bluetooth for short‑range device pairing. Mention security (WEP/WPA/WPA2/WPA3).

Tip: use quick live demonstrations — e.g., show a router status page, or ask students to find the MAC address on their laptop (Settings > Network > Adapter > Physical Address).

Phase 4 — Second Lecture (10 minutes)

What to teach next (deliver in ~10 minutes)

  1. Walk through the small network setup steps: physical connections, connecting modem→router→switch, enabling DHCP, and testing connectivity.
  2. Explain why firewall and user privileges matter — brief examples of what can go wrong without them.
  3. Show intranet vs internet vs extranet definitions and real school examples (internal portal vs public website vs supplier portal).
  4. End with quick comparisons: LAN/WAN/WLAN and when each is used.
  5. Finish by asking students to sketch a small lab network on paper showing modem, router, switch and a Wi‑Fi AP.
