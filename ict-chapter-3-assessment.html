Part C — Exam-Style Questions (Scenario-based)

Try full answers. Use the SUBMIT button for each question to reveal a model answer and marking hints.

Q1. A hospital needs to store thousands of high-resolution X-ray images for long-term archiving. Which storage medium would be most suitable and why? [4 marks]
Think: capacity, cost per GB, access speed.
Model answer reveals below
Model answer (4 marks):
  • Magnetic tape is most suitable (1 mark).
  • Reason 1: very high capacity — can store many terabytes (1 mark).
  • Reason 2: cheapest cost per GB so economical for long-term archiving (1 mark).
  • Reason 3: reliable and durable when stored correctly; sequential access is acceptable for archive use where files are rarely retrieved (1 mark).
Q2. A student frequently transfers project files (2–3 GB each) between school and home. Which storage medium would be most appropriate and why? [4 marks]
Think: portability and speed.
Model answer (4 marks):
  • USB flash drive or portable SSD (1 mark).
  • Reason 1: portable and easy to carry between locations (1 mark).
  • Reason 2: fast transfer speeds and large enough capacity for 2–3 GB files (1 mark).
  • Optional: portable SSD gives better speed and durability, USB flash drives are cheaper — award partial marks if justified (1 mark).
Q3. A movie company is distributing its new film to thousands of customers. Which storage medium would they most likely use? Explain. [4 marks]
Think: mass distribution, cost, compatibility.
Model answer (4 marks):
  • Optical disc (DVD or Blu-ray) (1 mark).
  • Reason 1: cheap to mass-produce and distribute (1 mark).
  • Reason 2: portable and compatible with most home players (1 mark).
  • Reason 3: capacity is enough for a film; Blu-ray for HD content (1 mark).
Q4. Compare SSD and HDD in terms of speed, cost per GB, durability, and portability. Which would you recommend for a gaming laptop? [6 marks]
Write a short comparison and a recommendation.
Model answer (6 marks):
  • Speed: SSD is much faster than HDD — faster boot and game load times (1 mark).
  • Cost per GB: HDD is cheaper per GB; SSD costs more (1 mark).
  • Durability: SSD is more durable (no moving parts), HDD is fragile due to mechanical parts (1 mark).
  • Portability: SSD is lighter and more compact (1 mark).
  • Recommendation: SSD for a gaming laptop because speed and durability significantly improve gaming performance and responsiveness (2 marks).
Q5. A bank wants to keep daily transaction backups for the next 10 years. Which storage technology should they use? Justify your answer. [6 marks]
Think: reliability, lifespan, cost, access pattern.
Model answer (6 marks):
  • Magnetic tape is a suitable choice (1 mark).
  • Reason 1: very high capacity to store large volumes of daily backups (1 mark).
  • Reason 2: very low cost per GB, economical for long-term retention (1 mark).
  • Reason 3: reliable when stored properly and suitable for archive use (1 mark).
  • Reason 4: sequential access is acceptable as backups are rarely retrieved frequently; tape libraries offer automated retrieval (2 marks).

Quick Summary — Storage Types

Magnetic: HDD, Tape — high capacity, cheap per GB, mechanical parts (fragile).
Optical: CD/DVD/Blu-ray — portable, cheap to distribute, limited capacity, easily scratched.
Solid-State: SSD, USB, Memory cards — fast, durable, more costly per GB.

Exam Tip

Always link reason to characteristic: e.g., "Choose tape for archiving because of its very high capacity and low cost per GB." Use keywords: capacity, speed, portability, durability, cost per GB.
