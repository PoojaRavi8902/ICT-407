Short Answer Questions

1. State two advantages of solid-state storage compared to magnetic storage.

Faster read/write speeds, more durable, portable, uses less power.

2. What is the difference between a hub and a switch?

A hub broadcasts data to all devices; a switch sends data only to the intended device.

3. Give one advantage and one disadvantage of optical storage.

Advantage: Cheap and portable. Disadvantage: Easily scratched and low capacity.

4. What is the purpose of a router?

It routes data between networks and connects devices to the internet.

5. Explain why Bluetooth is suitable for connecting wireless headphones.

It has low power use and works well over short distances (around 10m).

6. Define cloud computing.

Using remote servers over the internet to store, manage, and process data.

7. State one difference between an intranet and the internet.

Intranet is private to an organisation; internet is global.

8. Give one feature of a strong password.

Contains uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.

9. What does anti-malware software do?

Detects, quarantines, and removes malicious software such as viruses.

10. State one advantage and one disadvantage of cloud storage.

Advantage: Accessible anywhere with internet. Disadvantage: Security concerns.

11. What type of network would connect offices in different countries?

A Wide Area Network (WAN).

12. Why is encryption used during data transfer?

To keep data private and secure from interception.

13. Give one use of an extranet.

Allowing suppliers controlled access to company resources.

14. What hardware is required for video conferencing?

Webcam, microphone, computer, and internet connection.

15. Give one reason why passwords should be updated regularly.

To reduce the risk of interception or hacking.

Exam-style Scenario Questions

1. A school is deciding between using an intranet and the internet for sharing resources among students. Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of each option.

Intranet is secure and private, suitable for internal sharing; internet allows access anywhere but raises security risks.

2. A business wants to allow suppliers access to part of its internal network. Which type of network should they use and why?

They should use an extranet, as it allows external partners controlled access.

3. A company is choosing between magnetic tape and SSDs for backup. Evaluate both options.

Magnetic tape is cheap and good for large backups but slow. SSDs are fast and durable but more expensive.

4. A student downloads files from the internet. What security measures should be taken to prevent malware infection?

Use anti-malware software, scan downloads, keep software updated, avoid suspicious sites.

5. A hospital wants to use cloud computing to store patient records. Discuss benefits and potential issues.

Benefits: accessible anywhere, easy sharing, automatic backups. Issues: security, privacy, dependency on internet.

6. Compare Bluetooth and Wi-Fi for connecting devices in a smart home.

Wi-Fi is faster and covers larger area, good for internet access; Bluetooth is low power, good for short-range device connections.

7. An international team is using video conferencing for meetings. What are advantages and disadvantages of this method?

Advantages: real-time face-to-face communication, saves travel cost. Disadvantages: needs stable internet, time zone issues.

8. A bank is setting up authentication for its online system. Compare biometrics with passwords.

Biometrics are harder to forge and convenient but expensive and may raise privacy issues. Passwords are cheap but can be weak or stolen.

9. A university is considering replacing LAN connections with WLAN. Discuss the pros and cons.

WLAN is flexible and convenient, but slower and less secure than wired LAN.

10. An online business relies on web conferencing for staff training. Evaluate this method compared to face-to-face training.

Web conferencing saves travel cost and allows global participation but lacks personal interaction and needs strong internet.
